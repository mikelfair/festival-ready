Frequently Asked Questions

How do I write a compelling director's statement? +

A compelling director's statement should communicate your artistic vision, explain why you wanted to make this film, and give insight into your creative process. Festival Ready analyzes your project and creates a professional director's statement that captures your unique voice while following industry best practices used by successful festival submissions.

What should I include in my director biography? +

Your director biography should highlight your relevant experience, achievements, education, and previous work. Our director biography feature (coming soon) will help create a professional biography that presents your credentials in the most compelling way for festival programmers, whether you're an emerging or established filmmaker.

How long should a director's statement be? +

Most film festivals prefer director statements between 150-300 words. Festival Ready creates statements that are concise yet comprehensive, ensuring you communicate your vision effectively while respecting festival submission guidelines and programmer reading time.

What's the difference between a director statement and director biography? +

A director statement focuses on your specific project - your vision, inspiration, and creative process for this particular film. A director biography covers your background, experience, and credentials as a filmmaker. Festival Ready creates statements now, with biographies coming soon.

Do you create statements for producers and actors too? +

Yes! Festival Ready creates role-specific statements for directors, writers, producers, and actors. Each statement is tailored to highlight your specific contribution and passion for the project, using industry-appropriate language and formatting for your role.
